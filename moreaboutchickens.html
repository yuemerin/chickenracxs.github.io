How to differentiate between a hen and a rooster?

According to Chatgpt,

  1. Comb and Wattles:
    • Rooster:

      • Typically has larger and more brightly colored combs and wattles. Combs are the fleshy crest on top of the head, and wattles are the hanging, fleshy lobes beneath the beak.

    • Hen:

      • Usually has smaller and less vibrant combs and wattles.

  2. Feathers:
    • Rooster:

      • Often has longer, more ornate tail feathers. The neck and saddle feathers may also be more pointed and colorful.

    • Hen:

      • Tends to have shorter, more rounded tail feathers and generally less vibrant plumage.

  3. Size and Build:
    • Rooster:

      • Typically larger and more robust, with a more upright and prominent carriage. Roosters may have larger spurs on their legs, which are used for defense.

    • Hen:

      • Smaller and more compact in size, with a more subtle appearance.

  4. Crow:
    • Rooster:

      • Crows with a loud, distinctive sound. This vocalization is a clear indicator of a rooster.

    • Hen:

      • Generally does not crow. Hens may produce clucking sounds, especially when laying eggs.

  5. Behavior:
    • Rooster:

      • Tends to be more assertive, protective, and may exhibit territorial behavior. Roosters are often seen displaying and strutting to attract hens.

    • Hen:

      • Typically displays more docile behavior and focuses on nesting and egg-laying.

  6. Spurs:
    • Rooster:

      • Develops spurs on the back of the legs, which are pointed and used for protection.

    • Hen:

      • Usually lacks prominent spurs.

  7. Egg Laying:
    • Rooster:

      • Does not lay eggs.

    • Hen:

      • Lays eggs, usually with a consistent frequency depending on the breed.
